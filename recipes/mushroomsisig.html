Mushroom Sisig

Mushroom Sisig is a clever, vegetarian-friendly twist on the beloved Filipino classic. Traditionally made with pork, this version swaps the meat for finely chopped king oyster, button, or shiitake mushrooms, which are saut√©ed until they reach a perfect, meaty crisp.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 cup chopped red onion
  • 2 green onions
  • 4 thai chile peppers
  • 1 pound fresh shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 pound fresh oyster mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup calamansi
  • 3 tablespoons avocado oil mayonnaise

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion, garlic, and the white parts of the green onions. Cook and stir for about 3 minutes until they are fragrant and softened.
  2. Stir in the chiles and the chopped mushrooms. Continue cooking for 5 to 8 minutes, stirring frequently, until the mushrooms are tender and all the excess moisture has evaporated.
  3. Pour in the soy sauce and calamansi juice. Cook for another 5 to 7 minutes, stirring constantly, until the liquid reduces into a thick, savory glaze that coats the mushrooms.
  4. Give it a quick taste. If you want more "zing," add a splash more calamansi juice; if you want more depth, add a drop more soy sauce until the balance of salty and tangy is just right.
  5. Serve the dish immediately while steaming hot. Top it with a dollop of avocado oil mayonnaise for richness and a fresh sprinkle of the green onion tops for a pop of color.
